                              <p><strong class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px; font-weight: 700;">How will LG become "Supreme" regarding services matters in national capital?</strong></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><span class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px;">The ordinance has given the Lieutenant Governor the power to make a final call regarding the decision taken by the NCCSA regarding services. A constitution bench had curtailed the powers of LG in 2018, and the apex court had agreed with it. The Supreme Court had said back then that the powers accorded to the LG should be used only in rare circumstances such as finance and national interest. It had also been noted that the LG cannot refer every matter to the president.</span></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><strong class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px; font-weight: 700;">Why Are AAP and the Centre At Loggerheads Over Delhi Ordinance Row?</strong></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><span class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px;">Days after the Supreme Court granted the Delhi government the power to administer civil services in the National Capital in early May, the BJP-led Centre introduced an ordinance apparently nullifying the order. The order by the top court limited the power of the Lieutenant Governor over services related to three areas, such as public order, police, and land. However, on May 19, the President promulgated the Government of the National Capital Territory of Delhi (Amendment) Ordinance.&nbsp;</span></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><span class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px;">According to the Firestops, the Centre stated that the aim of the ordinance is to "provide for a comprehensive scheme of administration of services" that "balances the local and domestic interests of the people of Delhi with the democratic will of the entire nation reflected through the President of India".</span></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><span class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px;">As the Ordinance has been promulgated, the Centre puts the LG back in the driver's seat over the decision regarding the services. He will take the final call regarding every matter if there is a dispute between the NCCSA members.</span></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><strong class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px; font-weight: 700;">What's next in the ordinance game?</strong></p>
                              <p><br></p>
                              <p><span class="ql-font-popins" style="color: rgb(32, 33, 36); font-size: 18px;">The ordinance promulgated by the Centre can now be challenged in the apex court for a review, and the Union government will have to prove why it was necessary to promulgate this ordinance after the ruling by the top court. Another way out for this ordinance is to defeat it in the Upper house of parliament by seeking help from the opposition leaders. Arvind Kejriwal has met almost all the like-minded opposition leaders, and most of them have agreed to support him in this battle against the Centre.</span></p>
